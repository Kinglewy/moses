About Me

Visionary Ugandan Leader

Rev. Atuhaire Moses is a multifaceted leader whose life embodies service, integrity and transformation. His journey began in the remote village of Kyongera, where hardship was not just a chapter it was the entire book.

As a child, Moses battled malaria without nearby clinics and walked 14 kilometers to school each day for seven years, driven by a hunger for education and a dream of change.

To pay for his secondary education, he baked and sold pancakes turning flour and fire into tuition. As the only boy among eight siblings, Moses carried the weight of responsibility early, eventually funding his sisters' education while pursuing his own.

In 2007, his life took a miraculous turn when he was welcomed into the orphanage Bringing Hope to the Family, under the care of Faith Philo Kunihira, who paid for the medical treatment that saved his life.
